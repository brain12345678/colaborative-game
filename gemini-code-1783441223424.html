The core loop

Every island keeps two clocks.

One while the water's calm. One after it isn't. Everything you do by day is a bet on what's coming at night — and there's no one else to cover for you.

DAY

Reef, chop, haul

Dive the shallows for coral and shellplate, fell the last palms, drag scrap off wrecks caught on your shoal. The island only gets bigger if you're the one working.

DAY

Raise the walls

Stack driftwood barricades, sink a palisade ring, wire up a volcanic-glass alarm. Whatever you build today is the only thing standing between you and the tide line tonight.

NIGHT

The tide brings them in

The water glows, the temperature drops, and things that were driftwood a second ago start moving. Hold the perimeter alone until the horizon goes grey again.
