The core loop

Every island keeps two clocks.

One while the water's calm. One after it isn't. Everything you do by day is a bet on what's coming at night.

DAY

Reef, chop, haul

Dive the shallows for coral and shellplate, fell the last palms, drag scrap off wrecks caught on your shoal. The island only gets bigger if someone's working.

DAY

Raise the walls

Stack driftwood barricades, sink a palisade ring, wire up a volcanic-glass alarm. Whatever you build today is the only thing standing between your crew and the tide line tonight.

NIGHT

The tide brings them in

The water glows, the temperature drops, and things that were driftwood a second ago start moving. Hold the perimeter until the horizon goes grey again.

Gather

The reef is your quarry.

No forests, no mines to speak of — just what the water gives up and what you're willing to dive for.

01

Driftwood

Washes up daily. Your first walls, your first raft-dock, your first mistake.

02

Reef Coral

Hardens in open air. Brittle alone, load-bearing once fused with resin.

03

Volcanic Ore

Found only near the island's dead vent. Smelts into anything sharp.

04

Palm Fiber

Rope, sails, bindings. Renewable if you don't strip the grove bare.

05

Shellplate

Pried from the big mollusks in the shallows. Slow to gather, near-immune to claws.

06

Reclaimed Scrap

Pulled from wrecks the tide drags to your shoal. The only metal that isn't ore.

Build & defend

A base is just a bet you made about tonight.

Terrace the high ground, ring it in palisades, run a rope bridge to the lookout post. Nothing you place is permanent — the tide takes territory back if you let the walls rot.

What rises at night

They don't attack the island. They evict you from it.

Threat rises the longer you camp one shoal. Move, fortify, or eventually meet all four.

SPECIES 01

Tideborn

Wash ashore alone at the first dark. Slow, dumb, easy to wall off — until there are twenty of them.

SPECIES 02

Reef Wraiths

Camouflage against coral until they're inside your palisade. Post a lookout or lose a wall from the inside.

SPECIES 03

Brinehulks

Slow, armored, drawn to the loudest structure you own. Shellplate walls or a lot of running.

SPECIES 04

The Undertow

Doesn't come ashore. Just pulls the shoreline back a few meters, every visit, until there's no island left to defend.

Bring a crew

One island. As many hands as you can talk into it.

Split the work — someone dives, someone builds, someone stands the wall — or don't, and find out what an unguarded shoreline costs.
